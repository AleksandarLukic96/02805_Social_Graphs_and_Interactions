'''Robin Huntington Lee''' (December 2, 1919 in [[Saint Paul, Minnesota]] â€“ October 8, 1997 in [[Minneapolis]]) was an American [[figure skater]].  He was the 1935-1939 [[United States Figure Skating Championships|U.S. national champion]]. At age 12, he became the youngest skater to win the junior national title.<ref name="obituary">{{cite news | url = https://www.nytimes.com/1997/10/14/sports/robin-lee-77-figure-skating-champion.html | title = Robin Lee, 77, Figure Skating Champion | work = [[New York Times]] | date = October 14, 1997 | accessdate = October 29, 2009}}</ref> At the [[1935 United States Figure Skating Championships]], at the age of 15,<ref name="figuresinchicago">{{cite news | url =http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,770632,00.html | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20081231075618/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,770632,00.html | url-status =dead | archive-date =December 31, 2008 | title = Sport: Figures in Chicago | publisher = [[TIME]] | date =  February 22, 1937 | accessdate = October 29, 2009}}</ref><ref name="snowandice">{{cite news | url = http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,748489-2,00.html | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20091009214225/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,748489-2,00.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = October 9, 2009 | title = Sport: Snow & Ice | publisher = [[TIME]] | date = February 18, 1935| accessdate = October 29, 2009}}</ref> he became the first and, as of 2008, the only skater to defeat a seven time national champion in the United States.

Lee represented the [[United States at the 1936 Winter Olympics]], where he placed 12th. He was selected to compete at the [[1940 Winter Olympics]], which were canceled due to [[World War II]].<ref name="obituary" /> During the War, Lee served in the [[U.S. Navy]].<ref>{{cite news |url =  https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1338&dat=19460422&id=WiQVAAAAIBAJ&pg=7124,4709154 | title = Skating Expert With Ice Cycles Is Ex-Champion | publisher = [[Spokane Daily Chronicle]] | date = April 22, 1946 | accessdate = October 29, 2009}}</ref> After the War, Lee skated professionally in ice shows and worked as a coach.<ref name="obituary"/>

Lee was inducted into the [[U.S. Figure Skating Hall of Fame]] in 1995. He attended [[Erasmus Hall High School]].<ref name="snowandice" />

The Robin Lee Midwest Open is a USFS sanctioned competition held each year in the summer by Lee's home club, the [[Figure Skating Club of Minneapolis]], named in his honor.<ref>{{cite web|title=Robin Lee Competition|url=http://www.fscmpls.org/robin-lee-competition|website=Figure Skating Club of Minneapolis|accessdate=10 January 2017}}</ref>
